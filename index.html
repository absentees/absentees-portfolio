The Diner

For this college assessment I created an isolated abandoned diner. Deserted due to an unknown disaster, whoever left, left in a hurry.

Maya and Mental Ray were used for all components of this assessment with After Effects being used to add some music. Initially the project was to create a fly-through of the building, however for the mood and feeling that i wanted to provoke from this environment, i choose a high static shot to place emphasis on the diner isolation and loneliness.

Eveready Or Die

We were tasked for this college assessment to create a game using Flash for an existing brand or product. I chose to create a zombie-survival-side-scroller for Eveready Dolphin Torches. Click the image to open the flash file.

I took the opportunity with this assessment to have a try at a flash game engine I had been following for some time, Flixel. Originally created and used to create the wildly popular flash game Canabalt, Adam Saltsman create Flixel for rapid flash game development and I found it excellent to work with. I borrowed many elements from side-scrollers like Canabalt but also introduced some of my own gameplay elements like a torch and countdown timer. I also created all the art for the game.

Project Three

For this college assessment we needed to create a 10 second ident for our own personal brand. I created a small city around my design brand "absentees" that lights up and is readable from the sky at night.

This assessment taught me a lot about lighting, render times and how to optimise render times based on the shot. As i had around 200 "street lights", through trial and error i found out how to speed up render times by adjusting lighting settings whilst still achieving the desired look. This was one of my first 3D projects and I am still very proud of the result.

Office Gravity

This project was one of my more ambitious 3D scenes. I modelled, textured, animated and lit a 15 desk office and flipped it on its head, reversing gravity.

I used this project to learn how to use the render engine V-Ray. I quickly learned it is a very powerful and photo-realistic render engine, however not necessarily for photo-real animation. This hurdle taught me a number of new skills in render optimisation and using V-Ray's Sample Rate pass to identify problem areas in renders. Software used: Maya (modelling, animation), V-Ray (rendering), Mudbox (texturing, bump/normal maps), Nuke (Compositing).

Manhattan St.

Manhattan St is an old abandoned subway station, leading on from my Diner project. In a post-apocalyptic world the subway trains are still running and there is a man blind folded on the tracks.

I used some very obvious clues in this 3D scene to allude to the disaster that has or is about to happen. The billboards have messages in them, the newspapers have a photo of a nuclear explosion, and people have left luggage on the platform. In this edit i relied heavily on post to really create the mood of the subway, and quick cuts and some screen blur and shake created the uncomfortable and unsettling feeling of the subway. Maya used for everything here, Mental Ray to render and After Effects for post and compositing.

WOLF

One of my favourite stylised projects, i use one extremely bright directional light with all white shaders to create the B&W look and mood of WOLF.

The hunter and wolf is a very straight forward story, there's two sides to it and i wanted to express that not only through the characters but by the environment in WOLF. A single directional, highly intense light was used in WOLF to create long, sharp shadows and the B&W look. Atmospheric fog and z-depth passes were used to create an almost infinite depth and the ShatterIt plugin to explode the trees with the Hunter's bullet.
